## Chapter 1: The Tell

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words
**POV:** Dottie Kelly, third-person limited
**Setting:** Caesars Palace casino, Reno, Nevada
**Time:** Late evening, winter 1976

**Purpose:** Establish Dottie's extraordinary micro-expression reading ability and demonstrate the skills that will attract CIA recruitment.

**Beats:**
1. Dottie dealing high-stakes poker table, professional rhythm, reading every player reflexively—notices three players with synchronized tells (eye flick toward dealer when cards dealt, throat swallow before raising, pupil dilation when seeing specific cards). Recognizes sophisticated cheating ring working together.
2. **Dialogue Topics:** Dottie's professional dealer patter with players, terse technical explanation to security about cheating system, casual conversation with fellow dealers about the bust, internal monologue reading people constantly.

**Emotional Arc:** Professional confidence handling the table, satisfaction catching cheaters others missed, slight concern about making waves by reporting (working-class fear of losing job), ending with tired contentment—just another night dealing cards.

**Key Details:** 1970s casino atmosphere (cigarette smoke, polyester suits, disco music from lounge), Dottie's dealer uniform (white shirt, black vest, bow tie), specific poker tells described in detail, cheap Reno apartment she returns to.
